## Key Research Areas: From Molecular Genetics to Nanobiotechnology

Dr. Mohebifar's strategic research is founded on five main pillars, each addressing fundamental challenges in modern medicine. A detailed analysis of these pillars reveals the depth of his scientific perspective in deciphering cellular mechanisms.

### 1. Mitochondrial Genetics: Oncogenesis Dynamics and Chemotherapy Resistance

Mitochondria, as the cellular powerhouses, not only provide energy through oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS) but are also main regulators of reactive oxygen species (ROS) production and key apoptosis pathways. One of Dr. Mohebifar's most significant research areas is the role of mitochondrial genome (mtDNA) mutations in altering cancer cell metabolism and inducing drug resistance.

In one of the most comprehensive studies published in 2025, titled "Comprehensive Analysis of the Role of Mitochondrial DNA Mutations in MT-ND1, MT-ND5, and MT-CYB Genes," he investigated the mechanisms of progression and metastasis in cervical cancer patients. This study, utilizing advanced sequencing techniques on a controlled population, proved that mutations in the `MT-ND1` (encoding part of Complex I of the electron transport chain) and `MT-CYB` (related to Complex III) genes are significantly associated ($p<0.05$) with tumor metastasis and decreased chemotherapy efficacy. Biochemically, these mutations disrupt electron flow, and electron leakage causes excessive ROS production. Chronic oxidative stress in cancer cells stabilizes transcription factors like HIF-1$\alpha$, and by inducing angiogenesis and shifting metabolism towards aerobic glycolysis (Warburg effect), ensures cell survival in the hypoxic tumor microenvironment. Unlike the first two genes, alterations in `MT-ND5` showed no significant correlation with disease progression.

Furthermore, in another study, Dr. Mohebifar examined the role of point mutations in genes related to mitochondrial ribosomal and transfer RNAs, including `A1555G`, `A3243G`, and `A7445G` mutations, in cervical cancer patients. Precise genetic analyses indicated that the presence of the `A3243G` mutation in the tRNALeu(UUR) gene highly correlates with resistance to chemotherapy drugs. This mutation, by reducing the translation efficiency of mitochondrial proteins, modulates cellular stress pathways and raises the threshold for apoptosis induction. Hence, evaluating the status of these mutations can be utilized as a valuable biomarker in personalized medicine to predict treatment response. Conversely, `A1555G` and `A7445G` mutations were not effective as diagnostic biomarkers in this specific type of cancer, highlighting the complexity and heteroplasmy diversity in solid tumors.

### 2. Auditory Genetics and Mechanisms of Nonsyndromic Deafness

The connection between mitochondrial defects and sensory disorders represents another dimension of Dr. Mohebifar's research. His studies on nonsyndromic deafness reveal that although a major part of these disorders originates from nuclear genes, mitochondrial DNA plays a decisive role. A precise investigation of `A1555G` and `A7445G` mutations in deaf patients showed that the `A1555G` mutation in the 12S rRNA gene severely increases the susceptibility of inner ear cochlear hair cells to toxicity caused by aminoglycoside antibiotics. At the molecular level, this mutation causes the spatial structure of human 12S rRNA to highly resemble bacterial ribosomes. Consequently, antibiotics bind with high affinity to the mitochondrial ribosomes of the host cells, leading to halted protein synthesis, cell death, and permanent deafness.

### 3. Modern Phytotherapy, Selective Cytotoxicity, and Targeted Cell Death Induction

A fundamental challenge in modern oncology is the severe side effects of chemical drugs and their damage to healthy tissues. Dr. Mohebifar's research in phytotherapy focuses on identifying compounds with "selective cytotoxicity".

In a pioneering article scheduled for publication in 2026, he evaluated the effects of _Polygala tenuifolia_ extract on breast cancer cells (MCF-7 cell line) and normal epithelial cells (MCF-10A). This study focused on the novel mechanism of "ferroptosis"—a type of iron-dependent programmed cell death that occurs due to the lethal accumulation of lipid peroxides. The results demonstrated that the extract selectively reduces MCF-7 cell viability by 35.2% at a concentration of 100 μg/mL, while having no negative effect on normal cells. At the intracellular signaling level, this extract strongly inhibits the expression of the GPX4 enzyme (Glutathione Peroxidase 4) and the mTOR kinase pathway, while simultaneously upregulating the expression of the p53 tumor suppressor gene and the TGF-$\beta$ pathway. GPX4 inhibition led to a 2.41-fold increase in lipid peroxidation and the subsequent collapse of the cancer cell membrane.

In a series of other comparative studies in 2021, the cytotoxicity of hydroalcoholic extracts of Pennyroyal (_Mentha pulegium_), Summer Savory (_Satureja hortensis_), Peppermint (_Mentha piperita_), and Tarragon (_Artemisia dracunculus_) was investigated across various breast cancer cell lines (including MCF-7, T-47D, and MDA-MB-231). The presence of potent secondary metabolites such as pulegone, estragole, and rosmarinic acid in these extracts, by inducing controlled oxidative stress and caspase activation, triggers the intrinsic mitochondria-dependent apoptosis pathway in these cells, effectively preventing uncontrolled tumor proliferation.

### 4. Regenerative Medicine, Nanobiotechnology, and Acute Wound Healing

Healing damaged tissues, especially in acute burns associated with the destruction of the defense barrier and immune system, is another area of this researcher's expertise. Second and third-degree burns lead to a sharp drop in immunoglobulins, severe protein catabolism, and increased gluconeogenesis.

Dr. Mohebifar has developed innovative biological formulations in a series of _in vivo_ studies on animal models. In a study published in the _Journal of Skin and Stem Cell_ (2017), the therapeutic effects of a combination of sesame oil (_Sesamum indicum_) and pumpkin extract (_Cucurbita_) on third-degree burns in BALB/c mice were evaluated. Sesame oil, rich in linoleic acid and lignan antioxidants such as sesamin, stimulates fibroblast proliferation and reduces inflammation by neutralizing free radicals in the wound bed. Histopathological analyses using Mallory-Trichrome staining showed that using this multimodal combination creates a significant increase ($P<0.05$) in dermis thickness, restores tissue integrity, and induces hair follicle regrowth, leaving less scarring compared to standard treatments.

In addition to classical phytotherapy, integrating nanotechnology in biomaterial production has also been on his agenda. A study on the use of "biosynthetic Nano-Titanium Dioxide" combined with sesame seed extract for treating second-degree burns showed that the photocatalytic properties of titanium dioxide nanoparticles destroy the membranes of pathogenic bacteria and control wound bed infection. Simultaneously, sesame oil accelerated the epithelialization process by supplying essential fatty acids. This synergistic approach vastly improved therapeutic efficacy compared to common ointments like Mebo. Furthermore, in the field of nanomaterial synthesis, he has contributed to the sol-gel auto-combustion synthesis of Samarium-doped copper ferrite nanoparticles (CuFe2-xSmxO4), which hold broad potential in bio-photocatalysis.

### 5. Sports Immunology and Cytokine Regulation in Autoimmune Diseases

Understanding the link between lifestyle, the immune system, and disease pathology is evident in his 2026 research. Investigating the impact of 12 weeks of Pilates exercises on the serum levels of Interleukin-1 beta (IL-1$\beta$), a pro-inflammatory cytokine, and Interleukin-10 (IL-10), a regulatory and anti-inflammatory cytokine, in patients with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) represents an important step in clinical neuroimmunology. This study elucidates the mechanisms through which mechanical stresses and targeted exercises create a balance between Th1/Th17 immune cells and Treg regulatory cells, thereby suppressing neuroinflammation in the central nervous system.
